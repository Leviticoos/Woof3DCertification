Slicing Tolerance

One of the lesser known, yet extremely powerful settings at your disposal is that of the Slicing Tolerance. When you take a 2D slice of your STL, you really have a difference between the top of that layer and the bottom of it. The slicer can decide to fill that whole area, with a little bit of material going outside that bound, try to reduce the error, by having it print most of the inbounds, but leaving some areas unprinted to keep from spilling out of bounds too much, or do itâ€™s est to never cross that boundary. These options are called Inclusive, Middle, and Exclusive in most slicers, and are pictured below.



This setting comes in extremely handy when making parts that have to mate, especially when you want to print high tolerance alignment in place. The exclusive mode makes sure that any error in the model to print process is with empty space, not extrusion outside the bounds that can lead to interference between parts.
